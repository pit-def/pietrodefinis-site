Field research 2024 Alta Verapaz, Guatemala

Agua Segura

Citizen science Water stewardship Open data

A citizen-science program that pairs mobile labs, data stewardship and storytelling so rural communities can monitor and defend their water sources.

Overview

Agua Segura brings together municipal partners, youth leaders and health promoters to build a shared water quality monitoring routine. I led the field design, training and data components: adapting low-cost lab methods, creating actionable guides and ensuring that observations could flow into a public dashboard in Spanish and Qʼeqchiʼ.

Outcomes

  • Equipped six community teams with modular sampling kits and a weekly workflow covering grab sampling, reagent handling and safety.
  • Produced bilingual handbooks and a mobile-first data notebook so coordinators can archive and publish results without connectivity.
  • Co-designed a public dashboard with the municipal water office, visualising microbial risk, qualitative observations and maintenance status.
  • Supported a local storytelling sprint that documented upstream threats and secured maintenance budgets for four communities.
