Training 2025 San Marcos, Guatemala

Comunicación y Presentaciones Digitales

Facilitation Storytelling Capacity building

Two-day, six-hour workshop equipping fourteen NGO professionals with clearer storytelling, slide design and public speaking tools.

Workshop snapshot

Pastoral de la Tierra requested support to refresh how they communicate projects to communities and donors. I designed Comunicación y Presentaciones Digitales, a Spanish-language course blending theory, play and digital tools so the team could deliver concise, engaging presentations.

Role and responsibilities

  • Designed the entire curriculum, including theory modules, workbooks and interactive slide decks in Spanish.
  • Facilitated two three-hour sessions for fourteen staff members with mixed experience levels.
  • Developed group exercises using Mentimeter, paper prototyping and playful storytelling prompts.
  • Produced visual guides and infographics on effective presentation design for ongoing reference.

Curriculum structure

  1. Purpose first. Defined communication fundamentals using frameworks like Start With Why and key message pyramids.
  2. Playful practice. Ran group challenges such as defending Earth from penguins to train clarity, structure and confidence.
  3. Design lab. Guided participants through Canva and PowerPoint, focusing on single-idea slides and visual hierarchy.
  4. Reflection. Closed with peer feedback, personal commitments and printed guides to sustain the change.

Outcomes

  • Fourteen participants reported increased confidence and clarity in presenting to communities and funders.
  • Delivered workshop slides, exercises and printable guides that remain displayed in the organisation’s office.
  • Strengthened team cohesion by mixing ages, roles and communication styles in collaborative exercises.
  • Established an internal reference hub so future staff can self-train on communication principles.

Reflections

Designing a workshop for colleagues who usually focus on agriculture and land rights demanded empathy and adaptability. Blending theory with humor lowered barriers and turned communication into a shared game. The posters still hanging in the office are proof that clarity can be contagious.
