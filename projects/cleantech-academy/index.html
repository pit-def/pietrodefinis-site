PABAS Energy – Cleantech Academy

Cleantech Circular economy Systems design

Eight weeks in the Cleantech Academy shaping PABAS Energy, a modular system that turns textile and biomass waste into energy, biochar and carbon credits.

Project dossier

Project identity

  • Title: PABAS Energy – Textile and Biomass Co-valorization
  • Tagline: Turning waste into energy, carbon credits and learning.
  • Period: April – August 2025
  • Format: Online challenge backed by the EIT Deep Tech Talent Initiative
  • Type: Innovation challenge · Educational program · Sustainability

Partners & team

  • Partners: Cleantech Academy · EIT Deep Tech Talent Initiative · PABAS team
  • Focus: Modular pyrolysis and gasification concept for textile and biomass residues.

Role & contributions

Role: Project Designer & Team Contributor.

Responsibilities

  • Co-developed the PABAS Energy concept balancing feasibility, sustainability metrics and business logic.
  • Researched textile and biomass valorisation pathways to estimate yields, emissions and carbon-credit potential.
  • Crafted the pitch structure, narrative assets and communication strategy for the final review.
  • Coordinated remote collaboration, keeping momentum across time zones and disciplines.

Tools & methods

  • Tools used: Notion · Miro · Canva · PowerPoint · Online collaboration platforms.
  • Approach: Lean thinking, rapid validation and environmental impact analysis.

Motivation

I joined the Cleantech Academy to stress-test my ability to design and communicate a viable cleantech solution under pressure. What looked like a training program became a startup-style sprint that demanded curiosity, focus and stamina.

Approach & methodology

  1. Context exploration: analysed textile waste streams and biomass co-valorisation pathways.
  2. Collaborative ideation: developed a modular plant producing syngas, biochar and carbon credits.
  3. Feasibility framing: merged environmental, technical and economic perspectives for viability.
  4. Pitch & communication: balanced data clarity with storytelling to make the concept legible.

Team dynamics

  • Remote sprints with clear agendas kept delivery tight despite geographic dispersion.
  • Regular mentor check-ins steered feasibility and sharpened impact metrics.

Results & impact

Key results

  • Selected among the five winning projects of Cleantech Academy 2025.
  • Delivered a complete concept note and business case in less than eight weeks.
  • Established a roadmap for continued experimentation on the PABAS Energy hypothesis.

Impact

The project proved how educational challenges can seed real innovation, connecting circular economy principles with practical pathways for textile waste valorisation.

Learnings

  • Validation is a mindset: honest tests matter more than perfect slides.
  • Focused energy beats scattered ambition when working across parallel projects.
  • Remote collaboration needs structure, clarity and shared identity to thrive.
  • The best innovations begin as working hypotheses that evolve through experimentation.

Field story

Entering the Academy

Curiosity pulled me into the Cleantech Academy after months on a field project in Guatemala. The program quickly morphed from classes into a laboratory of speed and collaboration.

Building PABAS Energy

Working entirely online, our European-spread team shaped PABAS Energy as a modular system that transforms textile waste and biomass residues into syngas and biochar. I operated as the connector, aligning deliverables and translating complexity into clear presentations.

Winning through honesty

We presented the concept as a working hypothesis rather than a startup pitch. That honesty resonated with the jury and secured our spot among the five winning teams.

Beyond the program

The challenge taught me that innovation mirrors real life: messy, nonlinear, human. It reinforced that moving from hypothesis to experiment is the only way ideas gain substance — a lesson that keeps guiding the ongoing evolution of PABAS Energy.
