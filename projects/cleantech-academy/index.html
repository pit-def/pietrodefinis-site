PABAS Energy – Cleantech Academy

Cleantech Circular economy Systems design

Eight weeks in the Cleantech Academy shaping PABAS Energy, a modular system that turns textile and biomass waste into energy, biochar and carbon credits.

Program snapshot

The Cleantech Academy is a challenge-based program backed by the EIT Deep Tech Talent Initiative. Participants co-create climate solutions with industry mentors. My team focused on PABAS Energy — a concept using pyrolysis and gasification to convert textile residues and biomass into syngas, biochar and certified carbon credits.

Role and contribution

  • Co-designed the PABAS Energy concept, aligning technical feasibility, impact metrics and business potential.
  • Researched biomass and textile waste valorisation pathways to estimate yields, emissions and carbon credit scenarios.
  • Structured the team’s narrative assets — pitch deck, communication strategy and data visualisations.
  • Supported weekly coordination in a fully remote environment, keeping momentum across time zones.

Approach

  1. Context exploration. Analysed textile waste flows, biomass availability and policy incentives for decentralised energy.
  2. Collaborative ideation. Ran sprint sessions to map plant modules, feedstock logistics and revenue streams.
  3. Feasibility framing. Modelled energy outputs, biochar characteristics and potential carbon credit certification.
  4. Communication design. Crafted a story-first pitch backed by data sheets, FAQs and impact scenarios.

Outcomes

  • Selected among the five winning projects of the 2025 Cleantech Academy cohort.
  • Delivered a full concept note and business case in under eight weeks, ready for further validation.
  • Produced reusable communication assets for future investor and partner outreach.
  • Established an ongoing collaboration to test PABAS Energy beyond the program.

Learnings

Working remotely across disciplines reaffirmed the value of honest validation over polished slides. Keeping focus on one meaningful experiment at a time protected creativity and energy. Above all, the challenge reinforced that cleantech innovation starts as a hypothesis and grows through collective testing.
