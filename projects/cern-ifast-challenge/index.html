Co-leading a team translating accelerator technologies into a regenerative orthopaedics concept, from human-centred research to jury-ready storytelling.

Overview

I joined an interdisciplinary team of engineers, clinicians and business strategists to respond to the CERN I.FAST challenge. We mapped clinical pathways, identified how accelerator-derived tech could close gaps in bone regeneration and designed a concept that balanced technical feasibility with patient experience.

Highlights

  • Developed a diagnostic-to-therapy service blueprint, aligning the needs of surgeons, biomedical engineers and hospital procurement teams.
  • Ran rapid co-design sessions and prototyped scripts for physician and patient interviews to de-risk assumptions in under 72 hours.
  • Produced visual systems — from exploded diagrams to investor leave-behinds — that helped the jury understand technical nuances.
  • Captured field notes and data stories that fed into a final pitch deck, securing the team’s top-three placement.
