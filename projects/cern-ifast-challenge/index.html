X-Ray MAGIC – CERN I.FAST Challenge

Health innovation Design thinking Team facilitation

Ten-day sprint with The T.E.A.M. to imagine how particle accelerators can heal bones: I facilitated alignment, shaped the concept and delivered the final story in CERN’s Council Chamber.

Challenge

The I.FAST Challenge-Based Innovation program gathered researchers, designers and entrepreneurs at the European Scientific Institute to answer one question: how can particle accelerators improve human health? Our team — the Terrific Experts in Accelerating Medicine — turned that prompt into X-Ray MAGIC, an injectable bone cement activated on demand by low-dose diagnostic X-rays.

My role

  • Facilitated decision-making, retrospectives and daily alignment rituals across physicists, engineers and biomedical researchers.
  • Maintained the “So what?” lens that connected scientific feasibility with patient impact and jury expectations.
  • Structured the narrative arc, pitch deck and visual assets that carried the concept from lab notes to Council Chamber.
  • Co-led technical synthesis alongside subject-matter experts, translating accelerator principles into clinical language.

Process

  1. Framing. Reframed the challenge through interviews with mentors from CERN, HITRIplus and med-tech startups, identifying unmet needs in bone regeneration.
  2. Ideation. Co-created system maps and value propositions that balanced accelerator capabilities with clinical workflow realities.
  3. Validation. Prototyped interaction scripts, tested assumptions with domain experts and iterated on radiation safety scenarios.
  4. Storycraft. Crafted a multi-layer pitch that blended feasibility, impact and emotion for the final presentation at CERN.

Outcomes

  • X-Ray MAGIC recognised by mentors as the most feasible, high-impact proposal of the cohort.
  • Delivered a concept note and storyboard connecting material science, radiology and surgical needs.
  • Forged a network of collaborators extending beyond the program for future research opportunities.
  • Personal milestone achieved: entering CERN as an innovator, not a visitor.

Reflections

The sprint proved that innovation is a human chain reaction. Alignment and trust moved the project faster than any tool. Asking “So what?” at every turn ensured technology stayed grounded in patient value and societal impact.
