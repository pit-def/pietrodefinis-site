Operazione Caretta – Infiltrato al Nido

Environmental storytelling Volunteer collaboration Citizen engagement

A spontaneous volunteering experience that became a live communication system protecting a loggerhead turtle nest and the community that guarded it.

Field snapshot

When a loggerhead turtle nested near my hometown, local volunteers set up 24/7 shifts to guard the eggs. I joined their nightly patrols and quickly realised the story needed more visibility. In less than a day I built a mini-site and communication kit so people could follow the nest’s journey from incubation to hatching.

Role and contributions

  • Participated in daily and nightly monitoring alongside GADIT Termoli and Centro Studi Cetacei volunteers.
  • Built a real-time website in 24 hours to document updates, field notes and conservation guidelines.
  • Captured photography, video and first-person narratives that translated the quiet work of the team for the public.
  • Advised on ongoing digital strategy and began redesigning the association’s institutional site.

Approach

  1. Immersion. Joined patrols, listened to local experts and documented rituals around the nest.
  2. Rapid prototyping. Launched a simple, mobile-friendly website within hours to share live updates.
  3. Storytelling. Produced daily posts, images and short videos to turn passers-by into informed allies.
  4. Collaboration. Shared assets and guidelines so volunteers could continue communication autonomously.

Impact

  • The website became the central reference for residents and tourists following the nest’s progress.
  • Communication materials helped reduce beach disturbance and increased volunteer participation.
  • Strengthened ties between local associations, leading to continued collaboration on digital outreach.
  • Inspired further storytelling work to support marine conservation initiatives in the region.

Lessons

Operazione Caretta reminded me that communication can be an act of protection. By making the invisible labour of volunteers visible, the project transformed curiosity into stewardship. Small actions — one turtle hatchling at a time — can justify weeks of collective effort.
